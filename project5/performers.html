MEI LANFANG 梅兰芳 - Peking Opera

Mei Lanfang, born in 1894, was known for his female roles and was dubbed the "Queen of Peking Opera." He began his training at 8 and debuted at 11. He influenced Western artists like Charlie Chaplin and Bertold Brecht. During Japan's occupation of Beijing in 1937, Mei refused to perform, leading to his impoverishment. Post-1949, he held significant positions in Chinese opera institutions.

CHENG YANQIU 程砚秋 - Peking Opera

Cheng Yan Qiu started Peking Opera training at six, mastering various roles under different mentors. He debuted at 11 and gained acclaim a year later with performances like General's Son to Be Executed. B He joined Rong's troupe with a seven-year contract, learning roles like huadan and qingyi. Under Luo Ying Gong, he expanded his skills to literature, calligraphy, and other arts, enhancing his Peking Opera understanding. His unique singing and movements led to the creation of the Cheng School of Peking Opera.

MA LIANLIANG 马连良 - Peking Opera

Ma Lianliang was specializing in the Lao Sheng or old male characters. Starting his training at eight and performing by 10, he eventually led his own opera troupe. Drawing from the Tan School's techniques and incorporating other masters' elements, he founded the Ma School, which gained prominence from the 1920s to the 1960s. Ma's performances were known for their elegance and classicism. Recognized twice as a top performer, he also innovated Peking Opera in various aspects.

Famous Repertoire

CHANG XIANGYU 常香玉 - Henan Opera

Chang Xiangyu was a renowned Chinese opera actress. Trained by her father in Yu opera to prevent her from becoming a child bride, she gained fame at thirteen after performing "Romance of the Western Chamber," earning her the title "Queen of Yuju Opera." In 1944, she married Chang Xianzhang, who later helped in her career and co-founded the Xiang Yu Opera Troupe.

Famous Repertoire

Hua Mulan

YAN FENGYING 严凤英 - Huangmei Opera

Yan Fengying was known for her female roles and is remembered as the "queen of Huangmei opera." During the Cultural Revolution, she faced severe persecution, being falsely accused of "counterrevolutionary activities." Tortured and humiliated, she took her own life in 1968. After her suicide attempt, she was denied immediate medical treatment due to bureaucratic procedures and faced further indignities even in her unconscious state. Her husband, Wang Guanya, who also faced torture, wrote a biography about her in 1981 and later adapted her story into various media forms. Despite facing criticism for not protecting Yan enough, Wang remained devoted to her memory, never remarrying and dedicating his life to preserving Yan's legacy.

Famous Repertoire

Tian Xian Pei - Seventh Fairy

YUAN XUEFEN 袁雪芬 - Yue Opera

Yuan Xuefen, born in 1922, is considered one of the most significant actresses in Yueju opera's recent history. She is best known for her role in the 1946 production of "Sister Xianglin," adapted from Lu Xun's work. Before adapting it, she sought permission from Lu Xun's family. Yuan played a pivotal role in reforming Yue opera's staging in the 1940s, introducing innovative lighting, scenery, and softer costuming.

Famous Repertoire

Sister Xianglin

LOONG KOON-TIN 龙贯天 - Cantonese Opera

Loong Koon-tin, originally Se-To Yok, is a Cantonese opera artist who trained under several masters and founded multiple opera troupes. He has composed plays, recorded songs, and acted in stage shows and TV dramas. Since 2012, he's been the Artistic Director of the Yau Ma Tei Theatre Venue Partnership Scheme. Recognized for his contributions, he received awards including the 2013 Secretary for Home Affairs' Commendation and the 2016 Medal of Honour from the HKSAR government.
