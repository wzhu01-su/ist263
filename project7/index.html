WHAT IS CHINESE OPERA?

Chinese Opera is a performing art with a long history. Its prototype can be traced back to the Three Kingdoms period, roughly 2000 years ago. It was truly standardized by Emperor Li Longji during the Tang Dynasty. Different regions in China have their own unique types of opera, each reflecting their local characteristics.

The opera of northern and southern China has distinctly different features. Northern operas are primarily "multi-act", typically dividing an entire performance into four stages. In contrast, southern operas use a single main rhythm throughout the performance, making it more lyrical and fluid.
